About Wimbledon

Wimbledon is one of the oldest and most prestigious tennis tournaments in the world. Held annually in London, it attracts the best tennis players from across the globe.

History of Wimbledon

Founded in 1877, Wimbledon is the only Grand Slam tournament played on grass courts. Over the years, it has become a symbol of tradition, sportsmanship, and excellence.

Wimbledon has seen some unforgettable moments in tennis history, including historic rivalries, record-breaking performances, and moments of pure drama.

Famous Players

Many of tennis' greatest players have graced the courts of Wimbledon. Some of the most famous include:

  • Roger Federer - With 8 Wimbledon titles, Federer is the most successful male player in the tournament's history.
  • Serena Williams - One of the most dominant female players, Serena has won 7 Wimbledon singles titles.
  • Martina Navratilova - With 9 Wimbledon singles titles, Martina is a true legend of the sport.
  • Venus Williams - Venus has claimed 5 Wimbledon singles titles, cementing her place as one of the greats.

Wimbledon Schedule

The Wimbledon tournament usually takes place in late June to early July. Below is the typical schedule:

  • Qualifying Rounds: Early June
  • First Round: Late June
  • Finals: First week of July
